Final Academic Pursuit (Which may seems to be too ambitious, but I Deeply believed in.)

Lifelong

1.Moving Beyond Statistical Correlation, Toward True "Understanding"​ :I am convinced that mainstream paradigms like the Transformer represent a highly efficient "statistical shortcut" rather than a genuine "path to comprehension." My core pursuit is to conceive a novel architecture that enables intrinsic learning and autonomous reasoning​ in machines—a formidable, perhaps even radical challenge, but one I believe is essential for achieving true machine intelligence.

2.Abandoning the "General" Mirage, Embracing "Domain" Breakthroughs：​ I do not pursue the AGI fantasy of "one model to solve everything." Instead, I find the vision of Specialist Superintelligence​ both feasible and compelling: building systems capable of assimilating knowledge, refining reasoning, and ultimately surpassing the boundaries of human expertise​ within a specific domain—and doing so in an extremely short timeframe. This is not about generality, but about profound, vertical evolution.

This journey will be long and fraught with fundamental challenges. That, however, is precisely what makes it meaningful. Perhaps one day, we will make it a reality.
